Levisham is a picturesque little village set high in the North Yorkshire Moors National Park and surrounded by woods, moors and farmland. The broad village street is characterised by wide, grassy verges and 18th century stone cottages and houses.

Village Pub

At the top of the street is the local pub, The Horseshoe, which serves real ales and good food. Click here for more information.

Steam Railway

Below the village, on the edge of the moors, is Levisham Station, a stop on the popular North Yorkshire Moors Steam Railway. Steam trains run regularly via Goathland and Grosmont, some continuing to the seaside town of Whitby.

Picturesque Walks

From Lowstead Farm Cottage, a beautiful walk takes you through the wooded valley, past the mill and ruined church, to the neighbouring village of  Lockton. This is just one of many footpaths in the immediate area.
