Lowstead Farm Cottage is a conversion of eighteenth century farm buildings in the attractive village of Levisham in North Yorkshire. It offers spectacular south-facing views across the hills and wooded valleys of the North York Moors National Park to the Yorkshire wolds.

The cottage sleeps four and the accommodation comprises one double and one twin bedroom, an open-plan sitting/dining area, private south-facing patio, kitchen and bathroom. It is fully equipped for disabled guests.

Facilities include:

  • Combination microwave oven and four-plate hob
  • Refrigerator with freezer compartment
  • Open fire (with logs and coal) and storage heaters
  • Picnic area with table and seating
  • Free range eggs
  • Duvets, bed linen, towels, tea towels
  • TV and CD/radio
  • Off-street parking
  • High chair and cot available

Disabled facilities:

  • Accommodation on one level
  • Full wheelchair accessibility including kitchen and bathroom
  • Walk-in shower with wheeled shower chair and grab rails
  • Parking next to accommodation
  • Guide dogs welcome
  • Disabled guests need to be accompanied unless they can manage independently

Smoking is not permitted at Lowstead Farm Cottage.

Lowstead Farm Cottage was formerly known as Moonpenny cottage.
